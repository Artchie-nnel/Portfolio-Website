SDG

GENDER EQUALITY
Gender equality is a state in which people, Men or Women, have equal rights, opportunities, and treatment. It is a concept that aims to eliminate discrimination and unfair treatment based on gender, men or women.


Gender equality can be called: Equal Rights of Women and Men

SIGNIFICANCE

Providing women with equal rights and opportunities strengthens their ability to contribute socially and economically.


Economic development is faster and more equitable when women have equal access to education, employment, and business opportunities.


Gender equality helps reduce violence against women.


Incorporating women into the decision-making process strengthens democracy and provides wider representation in society.

Student’s skills, projects, or aspirations align with it

Education: Students can contribute to achieving gender equality by promoting equality access to education for all genders.


They may organize campaigns or projects aimed at combating discrimination and promotion of education for women.


Project: Students can develop projects aimed at addressing issues related to gender equality. For example, they can develop training programs for women in fields that are usually dominated by men.
